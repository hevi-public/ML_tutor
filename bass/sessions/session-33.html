The answer (only after your best attempt)

Why that worked

Transcription is the realest ear training because the stakes are musical: you want the riff, so the ear works. The method scales to anything — small chunks, hum before hunt, verify ruthlessly — and the humming step is non-negotiable: if your voice can't say it, your ear hasn't caught it yet, and your fingers will just guess.

The other half of stealing: saying it back. A fill is a short answer at a phrase's end — and the craft is placement, not pyrotechnics: fills live in the last bar of a section, borrow the song's own vocabulary, and always land beat 1 of the next section like nothing happened.

Building a solo? Same tools

A bass solo is fills grown up: a motif (steal one — even from your own transcription), repeated, varied, answered — question-and-answer phrasing over a form you never lose. Graded transcription targets and solo-building steps: transcription · improvisation & fills.
