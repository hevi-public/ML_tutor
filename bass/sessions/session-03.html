Session 3 · Phase 1 — First sounds

First frets, first landmarks

Your left hand has been politely holding the neck for two sessions. Today it presses down — three notes on one string, a riff that actually sounds like a riff, and a trick that lets the bass tune itself.

Tune up

Warm-up: the engine, crossing strings

One pass of Session 2's crossing drill — say "i, m" under your breath and don't let the alternation reset at the seams.

New thing: press your first fret

Look at the metal strips crossing the neck. Put a left-hand fingertip on the thickest string just behind the third strip — close behind it, not on top of it — and press with the finger's tip, arm hanging relaxed. Pluck. That clear note is a G.

Slide up to just behind the fifth strip: that's an A. Any finger that reaches comfortably is the right finger today — fingering systems come later.

Why that worked

Each metal strip is a fret, and each fret raises the string by exactly one semitone — the smallest step in Western music. Open E, count three frets up: E → F → F♯ → G. Two more: A. The whole neck is just that one rule, repeated.

The dots on frets 3, 5, 7 (then 9 and the double-dot 12) aren't decoration — they're the landmarks every bassist navigates by, and you just played two of them.

Tap anywhere — every fret plays its note. Find G and A on the E string, then peek at fret 5 against the open A above it.

Why the frets get closer together up the neck

Each semitone multiplies the string's vibrating speed by the same amount (about 1.06×), so each fret has to shave off the same fraction of string — and a fraction of an ever-shorter string is an ever-smaller slice. Equal steps of pitch, shrinking steps of wood. The full math lives in strings & fret math.

Apply it: the bass checks its own tuning

Here's the landmark trick: fret 5 of any string sounds the same note as the next open string. E at fret 5 is an A — the very note the open A string plays. If the pair wobbles, one of them drifted.

From now on you can tune anywhere: get one string right, walk the rest off it.
