New thing: the E minor pentatonic box — free with your bass

Five notes: E · G · A · B · D. On this instrument they practically play themselves — two open strings, three easy frets, no stretch anywhere. Learn it up and down until your hand stops thinking.

Why that worked

The pentatonic scale is a seven-note scale with the two leaners removed — the half-step neighbors that demand careful handling. E natural minor drops its 2nd and ♭6th, leaving 1-♭3-4-5-♭7: five notes with no half step anywhere, so nothing clashes hard against the chord. That's why it feels safe — not dumbed down, just de-mined.

The box you played is the open-position corner of this map. Same five notes everywhere — the dots are your playground.

The blue note

Add one forbidden fruit — the ♭5, B♭ in E's case — and minor pentatonic becomes the blues scale. It's a passing sting, not a parking spot: slide through it on the way from A to B and you'll hear fifty years of blues in one fret.
