Session 24 · Phase 5 — Styles

Rock: drive the eighths

The styles tour starts with the engine room: relentless, even eighth notes welded to the kick drum. It looks like the easiest thing in this course. Playing it great for three minutes is a real skill — today you start earning it.

Tune up

Warm-up: eighths on one note

Sixteen bars of open-A eighths at 112, strict alternation, dead-even volume. This IS the style — everything else is decoration.

New thing: the rock groove

Roots in eighths over A–D–E, with the two classic garnishes: a drop to the fifth in the bar's back half, and a two-note walk into the next chord. Note length is the sound — connected but distinct, each note handed to the next.

Why that worked

Rock bass is consistency as power: even eighths lock with the kick, note length stays uniform, dynamics sit at a strong medium with accents where the drums breathe. The discipline — changes announced on beat 1, one variation per section, never more — is what makes it sound huge instead of busy. Your Session 19 timing work and Session 18 note-length control just became a style.

Steal the feel: listening list

AC/DC "Back in Black" (the discipline), Tom Petty "American Girl" (eighths with lift), Foo Fighters "Everlong" (eighths as architecture). Listen for note length more than pitch — that's where the drive lives. More listening plus the pick-vs-fingers question: rock.

Apply it: three minutes with the band

The endurance cut. Start the groove machine, match its bass note for note, then carry it yourself. Three unbroken minutes — evenness is the whole test.

Band: I–V–vi–IV in C, rock 8ths, 112.

Wrap up

The engine runs. When your eighths stay even in minute three, that's not boredom you're feeling — that's what professional sounds like from the inside.
