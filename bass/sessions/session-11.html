Session 11 · Phase 3 — Theory in your hands

The major scale under your hand

Do-re-mi lives in a two-and-a-half-fret box, and your hand already has the finger system to play it. Learn the box once and you own it in every key — that's today's whole trick.

Tune up

Warm-up: the spider, briefly

One trip up frets 2-3-4-5 on the E and A strings — the box you're about to learn lives right there.

New thing: the G major box

Start on G (E string, fret 3, middle finger). Follow the tab exactly — the fingering under each note is the pattern your hand will keep forever. Go up, come back down, and sing the numbers "1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8" as you go. Yes, out loud; you know the drill.

Why that worked

You just played the major scale — the do-re-mi your ear has known since childhood. Its recipe is a fixed chain of whole and half steps: W-W-H-W-W-W-H, which on bass is just frets: +2 +2 +1 +2 +2 +2 +1. Start anywhere, follow the recipe, get sunshine.

The numbers you sang are degrees — they're how musicians name notes relative to home, and they're about to become your main language: "play the 5" works in every key, "play D" only works in one.

The whole neck in G major — your one-octave box is the bottom corner of this map. Roots are solid; tap around, nothing here sounds wrong together.

The two half steps are the flavor

Only two neighbors sit one fret apart: 3→4 and 7→8. Those two "leaners" are where the scale's pull lives — hang on 7 and feel it ache toward 8. Every melody trick and every mode you'll ever meet comes down to where those half steps land; the full story is in the major scale.

Apply it: move the box

The shape is movable — that's the superpower. Slide the whole pattern up two frets and it's A major. Start it from fret 8 and it's C major. Same fingers, same recipe, new key.

Test yourself: hum "do" on any random fret, play the box from it, and hear do-re-mi appear under your hand.

Wrap up

One box, one recipe, every key. And you're now singing degrees — the language the rest of this course (and every bandstand) speaks.
