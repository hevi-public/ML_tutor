Session 19 · Phase 4 — Grooves & lines

The groove lab

Time is the bassist's real instrument — the strings are just how you touch it. Today: three metronome games that build professional time, and the practice method that makes twenty good minutes beat two vague hours.

Tune up

Warm-up: the accent line

Root-fifth on A with beat 1 leaning — yesterday's ladder, one pass.

New thing: three click games

The metronome stops being a ruler and becomes a sparring partner. Game 1 you know (click = every beat). Game 2 — backbeat: halve the tempo and hear each click as beats 2 and 4, like a drummer's snare. Game 3 — half time: one click per bar, you carry the other three beats alone.

Why that worked

Fewer clicks = more of the timekeeping is yours. The wobble you felt in game 3 is exactly the skill being built: internal time. And the pros' vocabulary for micro-placement — playing a hair ahead (eager, punk), behind (fat, soulful), or on top (neutral, tight) of the beat — describes feels, not errors: chosen, consistent placement is groove; wandering placement is just late.

The other half of today: how to practice anything. The method is an index card: one goal per session, written down. Slow enough to be perfect. Chunk the hard two beats, loop just them. Raise the tempo only when it's clean twice in a row. That's the entire secret; everything else is consistency.

The 20–30 minute session shape

2 min tune + wander → 5 min warm-up/technique → 15 min the ONE thing on the card → 5 min play something fun so the session ends in music. Plateaus are normal (skills consolidate invisibly); practicing ≠ playing (both matter, know which you're doing). The full method: how to practice.
