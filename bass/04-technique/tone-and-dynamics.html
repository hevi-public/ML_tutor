Tone & dynamics — the sound between the notes

Two bassists, same bass, same line: one sounds like a recording, the other like a rehearsal. The difference is rarely gear. It's the five controls that live in your hands.

Dynamics are relative

An accent only pops if everything around it is quieter. Living at maximum volume ("digging in" full-time) doesn't sound powerful — it sounds flat, because nothing can stand out. The paradox of loud: play the non-accents softer and the whole line sounds bigger. f and p in written music are exactly this game, formalized.

The amp, briefly and honestly

  • Start flat (EQ knobs at noon), volume comfortable — fix your hands before your EQ.
  • Tone knob on the bass: rolling it down darkens; try it with palm muting for instant Motown.
  • If it sounds bad recorded, suspect (in order): muting, note length consistency, pressure/buzz, THEN gear.

The details (open as much as you like)

Palm muting — the third voice

Rest the edge of your plucking palm on the strings at the bridge and pluck with thumb or fingers: notes become warm thumps with a fast decay — the upright-ish sound of countless Motown and reggae records (palm mute). It trades sustain for punch; note-length control comes free because nothing rings anyway.

Consistency before color

Interesting tone is variation around a controlled center. The exercises below build the center: ten identical notes, then deliberate departures. Random variation is mud; chosen variation is phrasing.
