Getting a steady reading

  • One string at a time, plucked over the end of the fingerboard (a rounder tone reads better), other strings muted.
  • Let it ring — the reading settles about half a second after the attack.
  • Tune UP into the note (drop below, then come up) so the string holds its pitch.
  • Within ±5 cents the meter turns green — that's in tune for any practical purpose.
Why your phone can do this The tuner measures how often the waveform repeats (your low E repeats 41.2 times per second) by comparing the signal against time-shifted copies of itself — the shift where they line up best IS the period. The same math from the fret-math page then turns frequency into the nearest note and the difference into cents (hundredths of a semitone).
