Unit 6 · Styles

Funk — the sixteenth-note grid

Funk slices every beat into four and then plays hide-and-seek on the grid: a few fat notes, a few muted ghosts, and a lot of exact silence. It's the most rhythmically precise style on this site — and the most fun.

Before you start Counting "1 e & a", note-length control, and fretting-hand muting.

The three ingredients

  • The 16th grid — felt continuously ("1 e & a 2 e & a"), even though you play maybe five of sixteen slots.
  • Ghost notes (×) — fretting hand touches, doesn't press; plucking hand plays full strength. The "thunk" that fills the machine between real notes.
  • The One. Funk's law: whatever happens mid-bar, beat 1 lands HUGE. James Brown built an empire on it.

The groove

The ×-headed notes are ghosts — rhythm without pitch. Play along: groove machine — funk vamp on E7.

Funk harmony (mercifully simple)

Whole songs sit on one dominant-7th or minor-7th vamp — harmony parks so rhythm can drive. Your note pool: root, octave, ♭7, 5 — the minor pentatonic with the root doing most shifts. The complexity budget goes entirely to when, not what.

The details (open as much as you like)

Ghost-note mechanics, precisely

Fretting fingers rest on the string (multiple fingers = deader = better), zero pressure toward the fret; plucking hand strikes normally. The pitch disappears, the rhythm stays. Drill: four plucks alternating real-ghost-real-ghost on one fret until the ghosts are effortless. Then they start sneaking into everything you play, which is correct.

Where slap fits

Slap (thumb-strike + finger-pop) is a funk sound, not a requirement — Rocco Prestia and Jamerson played monumental funk fingerstyle. The technique gets its own treatment in the Expert unit; the grid and ghosts you build here are 90% of what makes slap lines work anyway.

Listening list

James Brown "Sex Machine" (Bootsy's one-emphasis), Tower of Power "What Is Hip?" (Rocco's relentless 16ths), Sly & the Family Stone "Thank You" (the slap prototype), Vulfpeck "Dean Town" (the modern grid, immaculate). Count "1 e & a" against all of them.
