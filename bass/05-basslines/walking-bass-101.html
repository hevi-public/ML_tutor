Unit 5 · Bass Lines

Walking bass 101

A note on every beat, strolling through the changes like someone who knows the neighborhood. It looks improvised — it's actually a formula you can learn this week, then spend years making elegant.

Before you start Chord tones and approach notes — walking is those two pages at a steady quarter-note pulse.

The formula (really)

For each bar of a chord, four quarter notes:

The beginner's recipe Beat 1: the root — non-negotiable, for now.
Beats 2–3: chord tones or scale steps heading somewhere (up or down, pick a direction per bar).
Beat 4: an approach note aimed at the next bar's root.

That's it. R–2–3–approach, R–3–5–approach, R–5–3–approach… every combination of "start home, travel, aim" produces a legitimate walking line. Swing the eighth-note feel later; make the quarters even first.

A written chorus to steal

Dm7–G7–C: watch beat 4 of every bar do the aiming. Steal this exact line, then vary one beat at a time.

The three classic bar-shapes

ShapeExample over CFeel
Scalar R-2-3-appC D E (F♯)smooth stroll upward
Arpeggio R-3-5-appC E G (A♭ or B)spells the chord clearly
Down R-♭7-5-appC B♭ G (F♯)descending gravity (great on 7th chords)

The details (open as much as you like)

Two chords per bar (the ii–V squeeze)

Real charts often put two chords in one bar (Dm7 G7 | C). Simple rule: root on 1, root on 3, with beats 2 and 4 as approaches. When in doubt, roots-only through fast changes is professional, not remedial — clarity beats cleverness at speed.

Making it swing (after it's even)

Jazz quarters aren't literally even — they lean slightly long-short with a fat, connected attack (that's the swing you met in the reading unit; the styles unit drills it). But the drill order is strict: metronome-even first, feel second. A lopsided beat can't be styled.

Where walking actually gets used

Jazz standards, obviously — but also blues shuffles, rockabilly, country two-beat turnarounds, gospel, and the last chorus of a thousand pop songs when the band wants lift. Walking is a transferable superpower: it's just real-time harmony navigation, audible.
