Things worth noticing while it trains: (1) The two accuracy numbers split — trained digits race toward 100% while never-seen digits plateau around 90%. That gap is Unit 2's overfitting, on real data, in real time. (2) Look at which red-boxed digits remain near the end: ambiguous 4s-vs-9s, 3s-vs-5s — the model's mistakes are largely the ones a tired human makes. (3) If your drawn digit gets misread, try drawing it bigger and centered: the model never saw off-center digits in training, so it has no idea what they are. Data is destiny, in your own handwriting.

What just happened

Nothing new — that's the point. This is the playground's exact recipe at 784 dimensions: a hidden ReLU layer finds useful strokes and curves (miniature cousins of Unit 5's convolution kernels — with the difference that a real vision system shares its detectors across positions, which is exactly why CNNs beat plain MLPs on images). Softmax + log loss grade the guesses; backprop assigns blame; mini-batch SGD nudges 25,000-ish weights, thirty-two digits at a time, in a Web Worker so your tab stays alive.
