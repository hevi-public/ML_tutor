The challenges

Anyone can press Train. The learning is in predicting what will happen before you press it:

  1. Prove the collapse. Ring dataset, 1 layer × 2 neurons. Train. It struggles — the boundary stays nearly straight-ish. Why? Too few bends. Now try 1 × 6: the ring closes. You just watched expressive power arrive neuron by neuron.
  2. Underfit on purpose. Spiral with 1 × 4. Watch the loss flatten high — the network simply cannot bend enough. This is underfitting, live.
  3. Beat the spiral. 2–3 layers × 8 neurons, tanh, learning rate ~0.5, patience. When the arms finally separate, look at the loss curve's shape — long plateau, then a drop. Training big models feels exactly like this, at 10⁹× the scale.
  4. Blow it up. Any dataset, learning rate 2. Loss bounces or explodes — the optimizer race's lesson, now in your own hands. Halve the rate until it settles.
  5. Same recipe, different soup. Press "new random weights" and retrain the spiral a few times. Different runs settle into different boundaries of similar quality — the rolling ball finds different valleys. Nobody trains a big network twice and gets the same weights; they just get similarly good ones.
