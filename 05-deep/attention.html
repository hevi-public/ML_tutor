Attention: every word looks around

The mechanism inside the T of GPT. One idea — let each word gather information from the words that matter to it — reorganized the entire field.

Before you start Embeddings and the dot product — attention is dot products between embeddings, plus bookkeeping.

The problem

The last page left a loose end: "bank" gets one embedding, whether river or savings. And meaning is relational in worse ways — in "the trophy didn't fit in the suitcase because it was too big", the word "it" means trophy, and you know that only by consulting the rest of the sentence (swap "big" for "small" and "it" flips to the suitcase!).

For years the standard fix was recurrent networks (RNNs, LSTMs): read left to right, carrying a running summary. It worked, but distant context faded — by word forty, word three was a rumor — and reading one-word-at-a-time made training painfully serial. Attention replaced the relay race with a conference call.

The idea

Attention lets every word, in one step, look directly at every other word and take a weighted average of what it finds. Per word, three moves:

  1. Ask. Each word broadcasts a query — "I'm a pronoun seeking my noun."
  2. Match. The query is compared (dot product — your old friend the agreement-meter) against every other word's advertisement of what it offers. Good matches score high.
  3. Blend. Scores become percentages, and the word updates itself with the percentage-weighted blend of the others' content. "It" comes out of the layer 60% flavored with "trophy" — effectively disambiguated.

Every word does this simultaneously, and the whole thing stacks: a transformer is essentially attention layers alternating with small MLPs (Unit 4's networks!), repeated dozens of times. No recurrence, no fading with distance — word 1 reaches word 4,000 as easily as its neighbor — and everything computes in parallel, which is why transformers could be trained on internet-sized text.

The math (open as much as you like)

The idea in words

Score = how well my question matches your advertisement (a dot product). Turn all my scores into percentages. My new self = the percentage-weighted average of everyone's content. That's one attention step, for one word; everyone does it at once.

Show the math
\text{weights} = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{\mathbf{q} \cdot \mathbf{k}_1,\; \mathbf{q} \cdot \mathbf{k}_2,\; \ldots}{\sqrt{d}}\right)
In plain English My attention percentages are: my query dotted with each word's key (a list of match-scores), scaled down a bit, then pushed through softmax — the standard machine for turning any list of scores into positive percentages that sum to 100%.

    The queries and keys (and the "content" vectors, called values) aren't the embeddings themselves — each is a small learned transformation of them. Those transformations are the layer's weights, trained by backprop like everything else. And each attention layer runs several independent sets of them in parallel ("heads"), so one head can track grammar while another tracks meaning.
